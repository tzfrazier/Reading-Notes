When to Use Interviewing

Interviewing is not always a good fit for the problem at hand. It is not well suited for gaining large quantities of answers, howerver it does tend to generate deep data sets about people's eperiences. They aren't good at predicting future behavior3. Questiosn that ask about future behavior will reveal current mental models4. Interviewing can be however used in combintion with other methods5.

To Interview Well, One Must Study

If you're going to have interviews that are useful, insightful, and/or persuasive, you have to do your homework.

The Impact of Interviewing

Interview data can change minds, and have a big impact.

Summary

As companies do more design work and strive for innovation more design research is needed and that should include interviews6.

  • Interviewing can be combined with other data sources
  • Interviewing is a skill, and takes time and practice to develope.
  • Interviewing can expose frames and models of thought. These can lead to new ways dealing with a problem.
  • Teams who meet their users will benefit.

Chapter 2 - A Framework for Interviewing

Frameworks are an important part of mastery. As you develope expertise in interviewing it moves from asking questions to a way of being.

I don't skate to where the puck is, I skate to where the puck is going to be. - Wayne Gretsky 7

Check Your World View At The Door

Try to approach the interview with a sense of bland curiosity. Because we have our own assumptions it often helpful to engage in practices to distance ourselves from them.

The Brian Dump

At the start of a project. Get people around the table (or asyncronous technology like a wiki) and get what's in their head out into the open. It's important to surface assumptions, expectations, beliefs, hypothotheses, and perspectives. This is not to correct them but to shake up and unsettle what's in our mind. Portigal recommends anonymizing feedback if possible.
